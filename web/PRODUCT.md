# Steamlink — PRODUCT

**Register:** brand (the landing site IS the product; a visitor's impression is the deliverable).

## What it is
A platform for **gasless onchain games on Base**, built on the Nexus engine SDK. A
player signs **one** ERC-7710 delegation when they join a room; every move after that
is gasless (no wallet popup) and every USDC bet is bounded onchain by spend caps. The
site hosts an in-built **games library** (UNO, Monopoly, more) served from Steamlink
itself. Today the library is a placeholder shell wired for porting real games in later.

## Users (two-sided)
- **Players** — want to play real games for real stakes without crypto-wallet anxiety.
  Primary audience; the page leads with them. Primary CTA: **play the live demos**.
- **Game developers** — install the SDK, define a game as data + Solidity, deploy to
  Base. Secondary track lower on the page.

## Voice
Playful, tactile, confident, warm. Three physical-object words: **sunny, toy-like,
hand-made-sticker**. The opposite of dark-neon-crypto and dark-arcade-gaming.

## Anti-references (what to never look like)
- Dark + neon "crypto" sites (the category reflex). Steamlink is bright and warm.
- Dark arcade "gaming" sites.
- Flat corporate isometric vector illustration (the 2018 SaaS blob look).
- Generic centered-stack hero with icon-title-subtitle cards.

## Strategic principles
- Emotional truth: "gasless, no friction" should *feel* light and frictionless, so the
  whole surface is sunny and unintimidating.
- The games library is the centerpiece and the conversion path. Everything funnels to
  "pick a table and play."
- Naming: public brand is **Steamlink**. The underlying engine/SDK is **Nexus**
  internally; refer to it as the engine where it adds technical credibility.
